Malala Yousafzai

Pakistani schoolgirl - Defied the Taliban and survived being shot in the head to became an advocate for women's rights, especially the right to education

About her life:

Early Life:

  • 1997 – Born in Mingora, the largest city in the Swat Valley in what is now the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Province of Pakistan.
  • 2007 – When Malala was ten years old, the Taliban began to control the Swat Valley and quickly became the dominant socio-political force throughout much of northwestern Pakistan. Girls were banned from attending school and cultural activities like dancing and watching television were prohibited. Suicide attacks were widespread and the group made its opposition to a proper education for girls a cornerstone of its terror campaign.
  • 2008 – By the end of 2008, the Taliban had destroyed some 400 schools.
  • 2009 – Malala started blogging about living under the Taliban for the BBC, she later became a national figure in her country, appearing on television as a spokesperson for girls' education.
  • 2012 – Malala was aboard a bus campaigning for education for girls in Pakistan, when the Taliban reportedly hijacked the bus and singled her out, shooting her in the head and the neck, she was only 15 years old.

Later years:

  • 2013 – She addressed the United Nations on her 16th birthday.
  • 2014 – Was person of the year on TIME magazine.
  • 2014 – In October 2014, Malala, along with Indian children's rights activist Kailash Satyarthi, was named a Nobel Peace Prize winner. At age 17, she became the youngest person to receive this prize.
  • Now – The Malala Fund has become an organization that, through education, empowers girls to achieve their potential and become confident and strong leaders in their own countries, funding education projects in six countries and working with international leaders. The Malala Fund joins with local partners to invest in innovative solutions on the ground and advocates globally for quality secondary education for all girls.
